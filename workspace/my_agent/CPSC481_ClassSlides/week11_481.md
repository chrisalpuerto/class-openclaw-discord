# CPSC 481 Week 11: Deep Learning for Perception & Language

## Computer Vision: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)
* [cite_start]**Problem with Flat Nets**: Standard MLPs ignore spatial relationships and suffer from parameter explosion with images[cite: 5912, 5914].
* [cite_start]**Core Mechanism**: Inspired by the visual cortex using local receptive fields and **Translation Invariance** (stationary patterns)[cite: 5919, 5920].
* **CNN Components**:
    * [cite_start]**Convolution**: Small "filters" slide over images to create feature maps[cite: 5925, 5926, 5928].
    * [cite_start]**Padding and Stride**: Control the dimensions of the resulting feature map[cite: 5937, 5938, 5939].
    * [cite_start]**Pooling**: Reduces computational load and provides robustness to small shifts (Max or Average Pooling)[cite: 5945, 5946, 5948, 5949].
* [cite_start]**Architectures**: Includes LeNet-5, AlexNet (the 2012 breakthrough), VGG, and ResNet (using skip-connections)[cite: 5959, 5961, 5962, 5963].

## Sequence Modeling: Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
* [cite_start]**Specialization**: Designed for sequence data (text/audio) where inputs are not independent; features internal "memory" or hidden states[cite: 5971, 5972, 5973].
* [cite_start]**Challenges**: Vanishing gradients cause RNNs to "forget" the beginning of long sequences[cite: 5982, 5985].
* [cite_start]**Gated Architectures**: **LSTM** and **GRU** introduce gates to control what information is stored, forgotten, or shown[cite: 5988, 5989, 5994, 5996].

## Natural Language Processing (NLP)
* [cite_start]**Word Embeddings**: Map words to low-dimensional vectors where distance equals similarity in meaning[cite: 6013, 6016, 6017]. [cite_start]Examples include **Word2Vec** and **GloVe**[cite: 6019, 6028].
* [cite_start]**Attention Mechanism**: Allows a model to "look back" at relevant parts of the input rather than just remembering everything; solves the sequence bottleneck[cite: 6040, 6044, 6047].
* [cite_start]**Transformers**: The basis for GPT and BERT; replaces RNN loops with parallelized **Self-Attention**[cite: 6053, 6055, 6058, 6060].

## Specialized Models & Ethics
* [cite_start]**GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks)**: Two networks (Generator vs. Discriminator) playing a game to create high-fidelity image synthesis[cite: 6062, 6063, 6067].
* [cite_start]**Transfer Learning**: Pre-training on massive general datasets and fine-tuning on specialized data (e.g., medical records)[cite: 6076, 6077, 6079].
* [cite_start]**Ethical Dimensions**: Models can inherit and repeat racial or gender stereotypes/biases found in training data[cite: 6093, 6095, 6103, 6105]. [cite_start]Toxicity and hallucinations remain major challenges[cite: 6107].
