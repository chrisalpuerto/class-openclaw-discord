## Acting Under Uncertainty
* [cite_start]**The Qualification Problem**: Logical agents fail in complex environments because there are too many conditions to list[cite: 6].
* [cite_start]**Numeric Degrees of Belief**: Agents use numeric values (0 to 1) to represent their belief in a state when data is noisy or missing[cite: 6].
* **Decision Theory**: Defined as **Probability + Utility**. [cite_start]Rational agents act to achieve the Principle of Maximum Expected Utility[cite: 6].

## Bayesian Reasoning
* [cite_start]**Prior Probability**: Unconditional belief before new evidence (e.g., $P(Cavity)$)[cite: 6].
* [cite_start]**Conditional Probability**: $P(a|b) = P(a \land b) / P(b)$[cite: 6].
* [cite_start]**Bayes' Rule**: $P(cause|effect) = \frac{P(effect|cause) * P(cause)}{P(effect)}$[cite: 6].
* **Independence**: Two variables are independent if $P(a|b) = P(a)$. [cite_start]This drastically reduces the parameters needed for the Full Joint Distribution[cite: 6].
* **Conditional Independence**: $X$ and $Y$ are independent given $Z$. [cite_start]This is a key development allowing AI to scale to massive symptom sets[cite: 6].
