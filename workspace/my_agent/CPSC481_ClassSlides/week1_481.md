## Defining Artificial Intelligence
AI is described through several key frameworks:
* **John McCarthy**: "The science and engineering of making intelligent machines."
* **Luger**: "The branch of computer science concerned with the automation of intelligent behavior."
* **Russell & Norvig**: "The study and design of intelligent agents that perceive their environment and take actions to maximize success."
* **Decision-Quality Framework**: Intelligence is defined as the measure of **Decision Quality** (how well a choice achieves a specific goal).

## Thinking vs. Reasoning
AI intelligence is split into two core mechanisms:
* **Thinking (Intuition/Association)**: Pattern matching using statistical correlations (Neural Networks). Fast processing but prone to **hallucinations**.
* **Reasoning (Logic/Deduction)**: Symbolic manipulation using rules and causality (Algorithms/Chain-of-Thought). Slower, sequential processing prone to **fallacies**.

## Intelligence Types: Static vs. Dynamic
* **Static Intelligence (Algorithms)**: Fixed rules and heuristics (like a GPS calculating a path). Provides the "guardrails" for agents.
* **Dynamic Intelligence (Deep Learning)**: Emergent patterns and weights (like a driver navigating by feel). Provides the "intuition" and adaptability for agents.

## Key AI History & Milestones
* **1950**: Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" (The Imitation Game).
* **1956**: The First AI Conference at Dartmouth; the term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined.
* **1980s**: The "AI Winter" period.
* **1997**: IBM’s Deep Blue beats world chess champion Garry Kasparov.
* **2014**: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) introduced by Ian Goodfellow.
* **2016**: AlphaGo beats professional Go player Lee Sedol.

## Designing Agentic AI
To build an effective agent, focus on these three components:
1. **Define the Knowledge Space**: What actions can the agent take?
2. **Select the Selection Engine**: Will it be static (if/then rules) or dynamic (deep learning models)?
3. **Validate the Quality**: Measured by **Accuracy** (did it solve it?), **Efficiency** (speed/compute), and **Robustness** (handling environmental changes).

## AI Industry Roles
* **Data Engineer**: Manages data integration, optimization, and quality.
* **Data Scientist**: Identifies use cases, experiments, and builds models.
* **AI Architect**: Governs and scales AI initiatives; the "glue" between teams.
* **ML Engineer**: Deploys models and ensures production readiness/feedback loops.
