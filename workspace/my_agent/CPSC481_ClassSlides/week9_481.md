## Evaluating Hypotheses
* [cite_start]**The Gold Standard**: **K-Fold Cross-Validation** is the standard for evaluating how well a model generalizes[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Generalization**: 100% training accuracy is often a "red flag" indicating the model has overfitted[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Tree Pruning**: A technique used to prevent overfitting by removing branches that do not provide significant predictive power[cite: 8].

## Performance Metrics
* [cite_start]**Confusion Matrix**: A table used to describe the performance of a classification model[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Precision**: Accuracy of positive predictions[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Recall (Sensitivity)**: Ability to find all positive instances[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**F1 Score**: The harmonic mean of Precision and Recall[cite: 8].

## Bias-Variance Tradeoff
* [cite_start]**Bias**: Error from erroneous assumptions (underfitting)[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Variance**: Error from sensitivity to small fluctuations in the training set (overfitting)[cite: 8].
* [cite_start]**Ethics**: A model can have 98% overall accuracy but 0% accuracy for a specific subgroup; evaluation must monitor impact, not just performance[cite: 8].
